* Overview
(site last updated {{{modification-time(%Y %B %d)}}})

I am a postdoctoral research fellow studying tropical overturning circulations
(i.e. monsoons, Hadley cells, and the Intertropical Convergence Zone).  I work
jointly in the UCLA Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences (AOS) with
[[http://www2.ess.ucla.edu/~mitch/Home.html][Jonathan Mitchell]] of UCLA and Westmont College and in the California Institute
of Technology Division of Geological and Planetary Sciences (GPS) with [[http://web.gps.caltech.edu/~bordoni/][Simona
Bordoni]].  Please see my [[file:research.org][Research]] page for more.

I am also interested in the [[http://www.openscience.org/blog/][Open Science]] and [[http://opensource.org/][Open-Source Software]] movements,
which manifests through developing open-source software tools for scientists in
the Python programming language.  Please see the [[file:aospy.org]["aospy"]], [[file:resources.org][Resources]], and
[[file:programming.org][Programming]] pages for more.

Please contact me (information below) with any questions about my research or
anything else you find on this site.  Thanks!
